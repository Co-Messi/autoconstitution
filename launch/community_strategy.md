# autoconstitution Community Strategy

## Goal

Build an open-source community around one clear idea:

> `autoconstitution` is autoresearch with multiple agents.

The community should understand the product quickly, try it locally, and then extend it with better constitutions, task adapters, and evaluators.

---

## Who the community is for

### 1. Builders

People who want to use AI to improve prompts, agents, and workflows.

What they care about:

- local-first setup
- clear examples
- practical reuse

### 2. Researchers

People interested in Constitutional AI, recursive self-improvement, and multi-agent systems.

What they care about:

- clean conceptual framing
- reproducibility
- explicit rules and evaluation

### 3. Contributors

People who want to add providers, task adapters, examples, and critique improvements.

What they care about:

- understandable code surface
- easy contribution paths
- fast feedback loops

---

## Community message

The community should not have to memorize internal architecture terms.

The shortest community-facing explanation should stay:

- one agent improves alone in autoresearch
- multiple agents improve together in autoconstitution

Everything else should support that sentence.

---

## What to encourage

- sharing edited constitutions
- publishing domain-specific task adapters
- comparing different judge / critic setups
- contributing better examples
- discussing where the loop works and where it breaks

---

## What not to optimize for

- vague “general framework” language
- enterprise-first positioning
- overwhelming newcomers with orchestration internals

---

## Best early community artifacts

- one great coding example
- one great financial-analysis example
- one “how I changed the constitution for my use case” post
- one benchmark or trace that is easy to discuss

---

## Community health principle

The healthiest community for this repo is one that treats the constitution, roles, and ratchet as first-class design objects — not just the code.
